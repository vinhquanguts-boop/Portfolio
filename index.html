Principles

Five operating principles for clearer interfaces.

01

Make the next step obvious.

A good interface should reduce hesitation. Users should understand what to do next without overthinking.

State: guided
02

Design feedback into every action.

When users click, submit, move, save, or decide, the system should respond clearly.

State: responsive
03

Reduce choices before improving visuals.

Beautiful screens do not fix confusing decisions. Structure comes first.

State: simplified
04

Treat empty states as part of the experience.

First use, failure, waiting, and uncertainty matter as much as the final screen.

State: pending
05

Design systems, not isolated screens.

Every screen sits inside a larger behaviour, service, and business context.

State: connected

Process

From messy situation to clear interaction.

A repeatable way to move from user uncertainty into screen structure, system feedback, and a clearer next step.

  1. 01Observe

    Understand what the user is trying to do.

  2. 02Map

    Identify steps, decisions, blockers, and system touchpoints.

  3. 03Simplify

    Remove unnecessary choices and reduce cognitive load.

  4. 04Prototype

    Turn the flow into screens, states, and interactions.

  5. 05Test

    Check whether users understand the next step.

  6. 06Refine

    Improve clarity, feedback, and visual hierarchy.
